\chapter{Theoretical Background}\label{chap:theory}
This chapter establishes the theoretical foundation for the empirical analysis conducted in this thesis by bridging the macroeconomic principles
of \gls{mp} with advanced computational linguistics. The first section explores the evolution of central bank communication. It examines
the mechanics of \gls{mps}, the distinct roles of prepared statements versus unscripted Q\&A sessions, and the broader ecosystem
of inter-meeting communication channels. The second section introduces \gls{nlp} within the financial domain, providing the
methodological background for extracting quanifiable signals from unstructured central bank texts. It covers the transition from traditional
dictionary-based sentiment analysis to state-of-the-art transformer models, such as BERT and its domain-specific adaptations.
The final sections outline the theoretical underpinnings of time series analysis and portfolio optimization, which are essential for the practical
application of the extracted sentiment signals in financial forecasting and the development of an investment strategy.

\section{Central Bank Communication}
Central banking has undergone a profound and irreversible structural transformation over the past three decades.
Historically, central banks operated under a doctrine of deliberate opacity. Before the 1990s, public explanations rarely accompanied monetary
policy decisions, and the prevailing orthodoxy among policymakers held that taking financial markets by surprise was essential
for the effectiveness of policy interventions. This period of institutional mystique, famously characterized by the "Secrets of the Temple"
mentality during Alan Greenspan's early tenure at the \gls{fed}, was defined by a distinct lack of formalized public guidance. Market
participants were routinely left to infer the target interest rate or the broader policy stance indirectly, relying on the size, timing,
and nature of open market operations conducted by trading desks \cite{NBERw11898}.

Since then, however, there has been a fundamental paradigm shift that repositioned communication from a peripheral public relations function
to a core, independent instrument of \gls{mp}. Public institutions have an inherent duty to share information, and central
banks have increasingly recognized that transparency and accountability are fundamental public service obligations \cite{blinder2008central}.
Beyond mere institutional accountability itself, the evolution of \gls{mp} frameworks—particularly the widespread adoption of inflation
targeting across advanced and emerging economies—has demonstrated that transparent and credible central banks are the absolute cornerstones
of long-term price stability \cite{daly2025dynamic}. Empirically, clear communication has been shown to enhance central bank independence,
strengthen institutional credibility, and actively anchor inflation expectations in complex macroeconomic environments \cite{filardo2008transparency}.

\subsection{Monetary Policy Statement}
At the operational core of the \gls{ecb}'s communication strategy is the \gls{mps}, which is delivered during the press conference that
directly follows the \gls{mp} meetings of the Governing Council. The council consists of the six members of the Executive Board
(including the President and Vice-President) and the governors of the euro area national central banks meet to deliberate and set monetary
policy parameters for the entire currency bloc. The \gls{ecb} currently holds eight dedicated \gls{mp} meetings per year, operating on
an approximate six-week cycle \cite{ITC_ECB_Schedule}.

\subsubsection{Day Schedule}
The organisation of the announcement day is highly standardized, reflecting the institution's strict commitment to predictability and the mitigation
of market volatility. Starting from July 21, 2022, the \gls{ecb} modified its historical publication timeline to optimize the way financial markets
process complex policy signals \cite{ECB2022_NewTimes}. The initial policy decision—usually a short press release detailing the exact interest rate
parameters, deposit facility rates, and main structural changes to asset purchase programmes—is published at 14:15 CET. Shortly afterwards, the press
conference begins at 14:45 CET, during which the \gls{ecb} President and Vice-President present the detailed \gls{mps} and interact directly with the
financial press. In parallel with the start of the Q\&A segment, the full written text of the \gls{mps} is published on the \gls{ecb}'s digital
platforms at 15:00 CET.

\subsubsection{Structure of the Statement}
The press conference itself is divided into two very distinct communication phases: the reading of the formally prepared introductory statement
and the subsequent unscripted Question and Answer (Q\&A) session. This structural division is not merely procedural; it has profound empirical
implications for financial markets, as the two sections serve completely different epistemological functions and generate distinct asset price dynamics.

The introductory statement is a highly structured, carefully drafted document that represents the absolute consensus view of the Governing Council
\cite{ECB_WP2742}. It offers a comprehensive, linear overview of the integrated analytical framework that progressively details the economic assessment,
inflation dynamics, and the evaluation of monetary and financial conditions. The language used in this document is deliberately precise, relying
on specific syntactical formulations to signal the future policy path—a practice commonly known as forward-looking policy setting
\cite{ECB_Statement_Oct2025}.

In sharp contrast, the Q\&A session introduces an element of controlled spontaneity. Advanced textual analysis and structural topic modeling applied
to the historical transcripts of \gls{ecb} press conferences demonstrate that the thematic content of the statement differs significantly from the
discourse generated during the Q\&A session. The topics that dominate the statement are persistent, structural, and strongly focused on standardized
macroeconomic aggregates. Conversely, the Q\&A often introduces completely new, emerging topics that were not addressed in the prepared text.
Empirical research reveals that the first six most prevalent topics within a vast sample of press conference transcripts are predominantly driven by
the Q\&A segments. This suggests that the true added value of the press conference lies largely in the Q\&A phase, which provides the public and
market participants with detailed explanations regarding the \gls{ecb}'s decisions in real time\cite{ECB_WP2742}.

\subsection{Other Communication Channels}
While the \gls{mps} and the accompanying press conference represent the focal point of the \gls{ecb}'s communication apparatus, they operate within a much
broader, continuously active ecosystem of publications, reports, and public engagements. To continuously steer the yield curve, anchor long-term
expectations, and fulfill its mandate of transparency, the \gls{ecb} leverages several supplementary communication channels in the weeks between formal
Governing Council meetings. This "inter-meeting communication" is a powerful tool for addressing structural complexities and financial transmission
mechanics that cannot be fully encapsulated in a single monthly press conference \cite{Istrefi2021ECB}.

\subsubsection{The Economic Bulletin}
The Economic Bulletin is a comprehensive, data-rich analytical publication that serves to fully explain the reasons behind the \gls{ecb}'s Governing
Council's monetary policy decisions. Published eight times a year—generally two weeks after the corresponding \gls{mp} meeting—the Bulletin
contextualizes the Governing Council's decisions against the detailed backdrop of prevailing economic, financial, and monetary conditions
\cite{press_to_speeches}.

\subsubsection{The Monetary Policy Accounts}
In a major step toward heightened transparency, the ECB began publishing regular accounts of the Governing Council's \gls{mp} meetings in early 2015,
aligning its institutional practices with those of the US \gls{fed} and the Bank of England. These accounts are typically
released four weeks after the corresponding meeting \cite{MP_accounts}.

The documents offer a detailed, synthesized overview of the Governing Council's internal deliberations. They outline the delicate balance of arguments,
the range of risk assessments considered by various members, and the different perspectives on the economic outlook. By documenting the full
range of analytical debates, the accounts help market participants understand the central bank's internal response function and the specific
macroeconomic thresholds that might trigger future policy interventions \cite{CorbisieroLawton2021}.

\section{Natural Language Processing in Finance}
The exponential growth of the volume of digital information has fundamentally transformed the methodology of financial and macroeconomic research.
Historically, empirical finance has relied almost exclusively on structured, quantitative data. However, language has always shaped financial markets,
and unstructured textual data—ranging from earnings call transcripts to central bank \gls{mps}—contains critical information about the future.
\gls{nlp} serves as a technological bridge, enabling researchers and practitioners to systematically extract structured, machine-readable signals
from these vast corpora of unstructured text \cite{IBM_IE_2025}. In the context of \gls{mp}, \gls{nlp} algorithms are used to quantify the qualitative
narratives of central bankers, capturing nuances in market psychology and institutional sentiment.

\subsection{Sentiment Analysis}
Sentiment analysis is the computational extraction of subjective information and emotional states from text, primarily focused on classifying
the underlying polarity of a document \cite{xing2020financial}. In general, this polarity is conventionally categorized into positive,
neutral, or negative dimensions. Early \gls{nlp} applications relied heavily on dictionary-based approaches, which classify texts by simply
counting the frequency of predefined positive or negative terms. However, standard baseline dictionaries routinely misclassify common financial
terminology; for example, the word "liability" is negative in a general context, but serves as a neutral, descriptive accounting term in financial
filings. This discrepancy has necessitated the development of domain-specific lexicons, such as the Loughran-McDonald Master Dictionary
\cite{LoughranMcDonald2011}, to accurately measure sentiment in corporate contexts.

\subsection{Transformer Models}
While domain-specific dictionaries provided basic insights, they inherently struggle to capture complex syntax, context, and linguistic nuances.
The introduction of deep learning, and more specifically transformer-based neural network architectures, has revolutionized financial \gls{nlp}. At the
forefront of this shift is BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers), a state-of-the-art language model that processes words
in relation to all other words in a sentence, rather than in isolation, thereby capturing deep contextual relationships.

Despite its power, general-purpose BERT is trained on standard texts (such as Wikipedia) and often falls short when confronted with the highly
specialized lexicon of finance \cite[s. 6]{BIS_WP1215_2024}. This limitation led to the development of FinBERT, a model fine-tuned extensively
on financial texts, which significantly outperforms general-purpose BERT in accurately classifying the sentiment of professional financial discourse.

However, central banking discourse constitutes an even more specialized field. As a result, the academic literature has recently introduced
models such as CentralBankRoBERTa and other Central Bank Language Models (CB-LMs). These models are trained explicitly on massive, specialized texts
that include central bank speeches, \gls{mps}, and macroeconomic research papers \cite[s. 4]{BIS_WP1215_2024}. Empirical benchmarks show that CB-LMs
significantly outperform both standard and financial models in understanding central bank idioms and accurately classifying the hawkish, dovish, or
neutral stance of official policy communications. By using these highly calibrated transformer models, researchers can extract extremely precise,
theory-consistent signals from institutions like the \gls{ecb} and the \gls{fed}, drastically improving the predictability of market reactions
and policy transmission.

\subsection{Topic Modeling and Feature Extraction}
Beyond simple sentiment classification, analyzing central bank communications requires sophisticated techniques to identify the topics that
policymakers are discussing and their developments. Information Extraction transforms unstructured, qualitative narratives into structured,
machine-readable data \cite{IBM_IE_2025}. Subtasks such as Named-Entity Recognition allow algorithms to automatically detect and classify specific
entities, financial instruments, or precise numerical values mentioned in the text.
